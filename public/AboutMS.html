What is Multiple Sclerosis?

Multiple sclerosis is a chronic disease that affects the central nervous system, which is the brain, spinal cord, and optic nerves. This can lead to a wide range of symptoms throughout the body.

Watch Video
  • Symptoms

    Multiple sclerosis signs and symptoms may differ greatly from person to person and over the course of the disease depending on the location of affected nerve fibers. Symptoms often affect movement, such as:

    Numbness, Electric-shock sensations, Tremor & Vision problems.

    Symptoms may also include: Fatigue, Dizziness, Tingling or pain in parts of your body.

  • Causes

    The cause of multiple sclerosis is unknown. It's considered an autoimmune disease in which the body's immune system attacks its own tissues. In the case of MS, this immune system malfunction destroys the fatty substance that coats and protects nerve fibers in the brain and spinal cord (myelin).

    When the protective myelin is damaged & the nerve fiber is exposed, the messages that travel along that nerve fiber may be slowed or blocked.
